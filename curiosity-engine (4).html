Our Workshops

Four Days of Discovery,
Hands-On Learning

Each workshop is a self-contained adventure. No prior STEM experience needed. Students compete, collaborate, experiment, and build their way to real understanding.

Day 1 — Engineering
🌉

Bridge Building Competition

Who can build the strongest bridge? Students go head-to-head to find out. Using materials we provide, they test their ideas, figure out what works (and what definitely does not), and learn real engineering concepts along the way. Spoiler: it gets competitive.

Day 2 — Chemistry
🧪

Invisible Ink Lab

Part science class, part spy training. Kids mix up their own invisible ink using real chemistry, write a secret message, and then figure out how to reveal it. It is one of those workshops where students do not even realize how much they are learning because they are having too much fun.

Day 3 — Space Science
🚀

Landing a Mars Probe

Your mission: land a probe on Mars without it smashing into pieces. Students design, build, and test a "probe" using the materials we give them, learning the same design process that real engineers use. It is a great reminder that failure is just another step toward getting it right.

Day 4 — Engineering Design
🎈

Build a Balloon Car

Students build balloon-powered cars from scratch and then race them. Simple concept, surprisingly tricky to get right. Kids prototype, adjust, and iterate until they have built something they are proud of, and then see how it stacks up against everyone else's on race day.

How It Works

Our Approach,
Step by Step

Every Curiosity Engine workshop follows a proven structure that keeps students engaged, challenged, and always learning from the first spark to the final reflection.

01
Spark the Question
We kick off every session with something that makes students stop and say "wait, how does that work?" Before we give any answers, we let the question do its job and get kids genuinely curious.
02
Explore Together
Students dig into the challenge together in small groups. Our facilitators are there to ask good questions, not hand out answers. The goal is to get kids thinking for themselves and actually listening to each other.
03
Build and Compete
This is the part where things get loud and exciting. Students get their materials and start building. Things break, get rebuilt, and slowly get better. That process of trying, failing, and trying again is exactly the point.
04
Present and Reflect
Every team shares what they made and what they would do differently. Learning to talk through your thinking and give honest feedback is a life skill, and this is where students practice it in a low-stakes, supportive setting.
05
Connect to the Real World
We close by zooming out. Bridge building connects to civil engineering. Invisible ink connects to chemistry. We want every student to walk away knowing that what they just did has a real place in the world.

Our Values

What Drives Everything
We Do

🧠

Growth Mindset

Getting something wrong is part of how you learn to get it right. We build that into every workshop so students feel safe to take risks and try again.

🤝

Radical Inclusion

STEM is not just for kids who already think they are "science people." We design every session to meet students where they are, whatever their background or comfort level.

🔥

Genuine Fun

If a student walks in nervous and walks out asking when the next session is, we have done our job. We build workshops that are fun first and educational second, in that order.

🏘️

Community First

We are proud to be a Mount Laurel program. Every workshop we run is our way of giving back to the students and families in our own backyard.
